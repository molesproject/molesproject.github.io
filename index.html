ABOUT MOLES

A "Mole" is a unit of measurement based on Avagadro's number. One mole is equal to 6.02214 × 10^23 elementary particles.

This project took me 45 minutes to make, so I made my calculations based on that time.

Electricity

My PC has a 500w power supply, but averaged at around 150w during my work.


1. 150 Watts × (3/4 hour) = 112.5 Watthours

2. 112.5 Watthours × (3600 joules/watthour) = 405,000 Joules

3. 405,000 Joules × (6.242 × 10^18 eV/Joule) = 2.528 × 10^24 Electron Volts


Summary: In the 45 minutes spent working on this page, my PC used around the amount of energy equivalent to 2.528 × 10^24 electrons or 4.2 moles of electrons passing through a potential difference of one volt.

Keyboard Force

The switches in the keyboard I used to create this site, made of high-density polyethelene plastic, have an actuation force of 45 grams. In total, 4573 key presses were made while typing the HTML code for this site.


1. 4573 keystrokes × 45 grams = 205,785 grams

2. high-density polyethelene is around ~300,000 g/mol

3. 205,785 g / 300,000 g/mol = ~0.68 moles


The total amount of actuation force in grams used to press the keys was around 206 kg, or the same amount of mass that would be present in about 0.68 moles of high-density polyethelene.
A single key with an actuation force of 206 kg would require a heavy blacksmith anvil to be placed upon it to type a letter!
